01 — what this file teaches

When the thing to explain is a conversation, not a table.

Manual, onboarding, "how this product works": in those documents the content is a sequence in time, and the KPI strip plus three-cell bento is useless. The right form is to re-enact the interaction and narrate each step beside it.

The simulator below replicates Slack. Notice what it does with the house style — and what it does not do. The rule is commented in the CSS and explained in references/layout.md.

02 — the simulator

Three scenarios, one player

Click the conversation to advance. Use ← → on the keyboard. Hovering pauses the autoplay.

03 — the anatomy

The five pieces

PieceRoleCommon mistake
Steps as dataAn array describes the script; the renderer is dumbWriting the messages into the HTML and losing replay
Narration alongsideExplains the current step without polluting the conversationStuffing the explanation into the bot's bubble
Pausable autoplayMoves on its own for someone just watchingAutoplay that ignores hover and runs away from the reader
Manual controlClick, keyboard, and step/scenario buttonsAutoplay only: re-reading a step is impossible
Style boundary--sim-* tokens confined to the simulatorLetting radius and bold leak into the page
Rule
Re-enacting a product is quotation. Quoting is not adopting.
